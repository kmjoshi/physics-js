Welcome to Physics with Matter-js! Learn physics by adding particles, moving particles, tossing particles and changing the physics of the world!

Motivation

There are many interactive, and otherwise, simulations on the internet regarding physics education. There are many guided courses with visual and interactive aids. There is still room for an open-ended learning environment (OELE) that combines constructionist pedagogy with various forms of scaffolding, found as best practices in review of OELEs. This is a proof-of-concept of an OELE on laws of motion and their scope from simple kinematics to many-body physics.

Key aspects of design that help foster self-regulated learning (SRL) (see report):
  • Activities that build on prior knowledge/interests, so the student does not ignore it
  • Activities should be sufficiently complex to allow for tinkering, so it is not solved instantly, allowing time to reflecton in the learning process
  • Presence of facilitators to drive interest, help students focus and reflect on their activities
  • Allows learner control: eg. ability to switch between difficulty levels
  • Provides feedback on learning: eg. in the form of an assessment
